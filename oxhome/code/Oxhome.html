Most of the girls specialize like this; becoming familiar with the vocabulary of an area, even if only in spelling, does wonders for both speed and accuracy of transcription. Words like "combinatorial" and "surjection" are burned into the nerves of your hands, though you couldn't possibly guess what they mean. And, of course, it has helped to become acquainted with the style of penmanship of the individual faculty. After the past few years, you'd swear you could write a letter indistinguishable from one by Dr. Chapman, the most prolific in the department. This relationship only goes as deep as handwriting, however. Campus couriers handle the delivery of manuscripts, so your knowledge of these men stops with the quality of their descenders and the tone of their prose. Dr. Symon is a patient and compassionate correspondent, but his handwriting is ambiguous at best, with scrawled letterforms that seem unwilling to commit to an identity. Dr. Evans's hand is precise and hardly needs transcribing; his words are just as exacting. Dr. Chapman is an effusive writer, smudged ink and forward slant belying his enthusiasm. When you had first begun your post, Ms. Sheffield tasked you with learning the name and position of all tenured faculty, so that you would not err in spelling their names, and so that you would assign the proper importance to their correspondence. You type slowly and carefully when a document comes from Dr. Blackwell. As chair of the department, his writing is frequently less technical than that of his colleagues, but his status demands extra deliberation. It would not do to irritate such an important man.The typing pool sits in the basement of Radcliffe Hall, located on the southern edge of campus. The aging building used to house the classicists, who departed the leaking building for greener pastures some years ago. Radcliffe now houses much of the clerical staff supporting Oxhome, with the women sequestered on the bottom floor. Despite its position on the outskirts, Radcliffe Hall isn't really far from anywhere; Oxhome is a dense place, and the department of mathematics is only a fifteen minutes walk away.
